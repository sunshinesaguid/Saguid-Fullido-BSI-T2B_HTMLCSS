BOAC CATHEDRAL

Boac Cathedral was one of the oldest built heritage of Marinduque province. It was constructed around 1590s, and was designed with earthquake baroque architecture. It is located on top of a hill surrounded by high stone walls and bulwarks. During the old times it was once a haven and a fortress of the natives. Inside the enclosed compound is the original site of the “Ina ng Biglang-awa” shrine. In 1579, a Franciscan missionary, Fray Estevan Ortiz, planted the first cross on Marinduque island. After a year, the first visita that was called “Monserrat de Marinduque” and now is the municipality of Boac, was established with Fray Alonzo Banol as its minister. It was 1613 when the administration of the church was handed over to the Archbishop of Manila by Miguel Garcia Serrano who then entrusted the island to the Society of Jesus. The Jesuits assumed the spiritual administration of the island in 1621.

The townspeople considered the cathedral not only as a worshiping house but also a refuge whenever the town was being attacked by the Moro pirates. The townspeople would pray hard to their patroness, Our Lady of Immaculate Conception to save them from the attack. They believed that she will protect them. Moro pirates in those days frequently invaded the shores of Boac through the mouth of the river in Brgy. Laylay. In one attempt to conquer the town of Boac, the invaders were determined to kill many native defenders. When suddenly a powerful storm came. The people saw a glowing apparition of a beautiful lady with arms wide open and standing on the southern side of the fortress wall of the church. The scene made the invaders fled away. Since that incident, “Ina ng Biglang Awa” was created as a new name for the lady as an honor given by the people.

A force composed of Filipino revolutionaries, known as the Pulahanes, led by Maximo Melencio, launched an offensive against the American garrison stationed in the town. Armed with traditional bolos and improvised weapons, the Pulahanes bravely engaged the better-equipped American soldiers in fierce combat.

In 1792, a marker on the church’s front wall declares “naniniwala ang mga tao na siya ang nagligtas sa pagsalakay ng mga moro noong ika-18 dantaon.” As a dedication and gratefulness to the Ina of Biglang Awa. The canonical coronation of the miraculous object of veneration took place in Boac on May 10, 1958 to mark its 100th anniversary. It was attended by thousands of pilgrims from the nearby provinces in southern Luzon and Bicol. On the 11th day of the month of November, is her feast day falls on. Now, in the present days, the cathedral still remains as one of the landmark of the province of Marinduque. Some of the interiors have been preserved. And it is where the Moriones Festival is being celebrated.

The people of the province still preserves and protect the cathedral. And the legend of the cathedral is still being passed on generations to not forgot to be grateful and to keep the history alive.
